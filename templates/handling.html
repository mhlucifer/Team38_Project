How to Stop the Spread of Cane Toads

Follow these steps to help control the population of cane toads in your area:

  • Do not leave pet food laying around in the yard.
  • Remove any unnecessary water from your yard.
  • Clear up hiding spots by removing rubbish.
  • Turn outside lights off to avoid attracting insects.
  • Trapping and removing eggs and toad tadpoles is effective.

How to Humanely Kill Toads

  • Do not be cruel - treat all animals humanely.
  • Use a cane toad spray from a hardware store.
  • Stepped hypothermia: refrigerate for 10-12 hours.

What to do when you see a Cane Toad (Step by Step Guide)

Stay Calm

If you encounter a cane toad, remain calm and avoid panicking. They are generally harmless if not provoked

Observe from a Distance

Stand back and observe the cane toad from a safe distance. Do not attempt to approach or touch the toad, as they can secrete toxic substances that may harm you or your pets

Do Not Touch

Resist the temptation to touch or handle the cane toad. Even though they may appear harmless, their toxic secretions can cause irritation or more severe reactions if they come into contact with your skin or eyes

Keep Pets Away

If you have pets with you, ensure they are kept at a safe distance from the cane toad. Pets may be curious and attempt to investigate or even ingest the toad, which can lead to poisoning and health complications

Avoid Provocation

Do not attempt to provoke or disturb the cane toad in any way. Provoking the toad may cause it to feel threatened and release its toxins as a defence mechanism, increasing the risk of exposure to you and your pets

Notify Authorities

If you encounter a cane toad in a public area or suspect an infestation in your neighbourhood, consider notifying local authorities or environmental agencies. They can provide guidance on how to safely manage the situation and prevent further spread

Learn More

Take this opportunity to learn more about cane toads and their behaviour. Understanding their habits and habitat preferences will help you avoid future encounters and contribute to efforts aimed at managing their populations

Spread Awareness

Share your knowledge and experience with friends, family, and classmates. By raising awareness about the presence of cane toads and the importance of responsible behaviour, you can help protect local ecosystems and wildlife
