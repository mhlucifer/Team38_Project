Our Step Towards a Cane Toad Free Environment

Our Impact

Cane toads pose a multifaceted threat to biodiversity due to their poisonous nature, predatory behaviour, adaptability, and competitiveness. Toxic at all life stages, their ingestion has led to the decline and extinction of numerous native predator species in regions like the Northern Territory and Queensland by up to 80%. Their huge appetite spans from insects to small mammals, exacerbating competition for resources and depleting native species' food sources. Thriving in diverse habitats, including urban areas, and reproducing rapidly, cane toads have become a difficult invasive species to handle that are capable of outcompeting and dominating ecosystems at an unmatched rate.
